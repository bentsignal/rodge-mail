# Rodge Mail logo exploration — round two

These five marks intentionally leave the previous folded-monogram and envelope
territory. They are early identity studies, not production assets.

## 01 — Switchboard

A routing junction for the **Dispatch** direction. Three inputs become one
decisive output, expressing unified accounts, control, and speed. The transit-
sign geometry and cobalt/vermilion palette make this the most operational mark.

## 02 — Between Lines

Two human ink gestures hold a point of attention between them. This maps to
**Correspondence** without illustrating a letter, seal, or mailbox. It is the
warmest and most literary direction.

## 03 — Live Signal

A restrained stream becomes one confident pulse. It belongs naturally to
**Frequency** and gives the product a dark-first, technical identity. This is
the sharpest and most immediately legible mark of the set.

## 04 — Twin Orbit

Two unequal account identities move around shared open space without merging.
This maps directly to **Orbit** and favors a tactile, mobile-first personality.
The asymmetry and soft geometry deliberately contrast with the operations mark.

## 05 — Night Firefly

A wildcard mascot built around the product promise: find the small number of
messages that deserve attention. It avoids mail iconography entirely and could
support a more personal, characterful rebrand across loading, empty, and AI
states.

## Review notes

- The symbols use five different organizing ideas: junction, gesture, signal,
  spatial system, and mascot.
- No direction uses an `R`, `H`, envelope, folded paper, paper plane, or wax
  seal.
- The supplied PNGs are generated concept art. A selected direction would need
  a deterministic vector redraw, optical-size pass, app-icon masks, monochrome
  variants, and light/dark production testing.
- Nothing in this directory is wired into the shipping applications.
